Abrasive Blasting for Construction Companies

Equipment cleaning, site surface preparation, and structural steel restoration — on-site at your yard or job site.

Construction equipment takes a beating. Every job site deposits another layer of caked concrete, mud, rust, and coating damage on your machines. Between projects, that buildup needs to come off — not just for appearances, but to inspect for structural damage, prepare for repainting, or get equipment ready for resale or lease return. Hand-cleaning is slow and incomplete. Pressure washing moves the dirt around but doesn't remove rust or failing coatings. Abrasive blasting strips everything back to clean metal in a fraction of the time.

We bring our DB500 mobile blasting system directly to your equipment yard, staging area, or active job site. No flatbed hauling, no off-site shop delays, no downtime waiting for machines to come back. We set up alongside your fleet and work through it systematically — excavators, dozers, loaders, dump trucks, trailers, whatever needs cleaning.

The Problem

Why Construction Companies Need Mobile Blasting

The math on construction equipment maintenance is straightforward. A single excavator sitting in a yard for three months in Georgia's climate develops visible surface rust on every exposed metal surface. Leave it longer, and that rust becomes structural. Repainting over rust traps moisture underneath and accelerates the problem. The only real fix is removing the rust entirely, and the only efficient way to do that on a 60,000-pound machine is abrasive blasting.

The alternative — hauling equipment to an off-site shop — costs time and money before the actual blasting even starts. Flatbed transport, scheduling delays, and turnaround time add up. With our mobile setup, we come to your yard and blast while your crew handles other work. A piece of equipment that would take a week round-trip to an off-site shop gets done in a day on-site.

For GCs and specialty contractors working renovation and adaptive reuse projects, we handle the surface prep side of demolition — stripping old coatings from steel, concrete, and masonry so the next trade can work on clean, properly prepared surfaces. This is faster and more thorough than manual methods and creates a better substrate for whatever coating or finish comes next.

FAQ

Construction Blasting — Common Questions

Can you clean construction equipment on-site?

Yes. Our DB500 comes to your equipment yard, job site, or staging area. We clean excavators, dozers, loaders, dump trucks, trailers, and other heavy equipment in place — no hauling required.

What does blasting do for construction equipment?

Removes rust, caked concrete and mud, old paint, grease buildup, and surface contamination from frames, buckets, booms, beds, and undercarriages. Restores equipment to clean metal for repainting, resale, or corrosion protection.

Do you blast structural steel on construction sites?

Yes. We blast steel to SSPC standards on-site before primer and paint application. Proper surface preparation ensures coatings bond correctly and last their full rated lifespan.

Can you profile new concrete before coatings?

Yes. New concrete needs proper surface profiling before epoxy or polyurethane coatings. We blast to the manufacturer-specified CSP level so coatings adhere properly from day one.
