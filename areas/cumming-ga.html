Local Expertise

Why Cumming & Forsyth County Need Surface Preparation

Forsyth County's growth creates a steady cycle of surface maintenance demand. New commercial developments along GA-400 need concrete profiling before coatings can be applied. Aging strip malls and office parks along Tribble Gap Road and Bethelview Road need exterior coating removal and repainting prep. The county's massive residential construction pipeline — thousands of new homes in developments like Vickery, Sharon Springs, and throughout the Big Creek and Silver City communities — generates demand for residential metal restoration, fence and railing cleanup, and concrete surface work.

Major employers like Scientific Games, Tyson Foods, and Siemens Industry maintain facilities in the area that depend on properly maintained infrastructure. The industrial operations around downtown Cumming — including Tyson's processing plant that's been operating since the 1950s — require ongoing rust remediation, equipment cleaning, and structural steel maintenance.

And on the eastern edge of the county, Lake Lanier's shoreline creates the same moisture-driven corrosion problems that affect every lakefront community in the region. Docks, boat lifts, seawalls, and outdoor metal structures corrode faster here than inland, and mobile blasting is the most practical way to address it without transporting anything off-property.
