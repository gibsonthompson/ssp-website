Why Flowery Branch Properties Need Surface Preparation

Flowery Branch's location on Lake Lanier creates a unique set of surface maintenance challenges that inland communities don't face. The constant moisture and humidity off the lake accelerates rust formation on exposed metal — dock hardware, railings, outdoor fixtures, boat lifts, and metal roofing all deteriorate faster here than they would just a few miles from the water. Protective coatings break down sooner, and concrete surfaces near the shoreline absorb moisture that leads to spalling and coating adhesion failures.

On the commercial and industrial side, Flowery Branch's growth along the Atlanta Highway corridor and Spout Springs Road has brought new warehouse space, manufacturing facilities, and retail developments that all require surface maintenance. Pattillo Industrial Real Estate is developing a 200,000-square-foot facility in Flowery Branch. King's Hawaiian operates a 116,000-square-foot bakery and distribution center here. Mars Wrigley manufactures products in the area. These facilities and their supporting infrastructure need regular rust remediation, coating maintenance, and concrete floor preparation.

Mobile blasting makes particular sense in Flowery Branch because many of the properties that need surface work — lakefront docks, residential metal structures, commercial building exteriors — can't easily be transported to a shop. We come to you, set up on-site, and handle the job without disrupting your home, business, or tenants.
