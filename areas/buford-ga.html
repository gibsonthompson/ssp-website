Why Buford Properties Need Surface Preparation

Buford has been an industrial city since the 1870s. The leather tanning trade that earned it the nickname "The Leather City" is long gone, but the manufacturing DNA remains. Makita operates a factory here with 400 employees. Lund International runs a 219,000-square-foot headquarters and a 193,000-square-foot warehouse. CAB Incorporated manufactures steel pipe flanges. E-commerce fulfillment and distribution centers continue to expand along I-85. All of these operations depend on maintained infrastructure — steel that resists corrosion, concrete floors with proper surface profile, and equipment that stays clean and functional.

Buford's position on the northeast industrial corridor — one of metro Atlanta's most active zones for warehousing and distribution with roughly 130 million square feet of industrial space — means there's a massive base of concrete, steel, and coated surfaces that need regular professional maintenance. Add the moisture and humidity from Lake Lanier on the northern edge of the city, and you get accelerated corrosion on everything from lakefront dock hardware to parking lot fixtures at commercial properties near the Mall of Georgia.

Our mobile blasting service is built for exactly this environment. We handle industrial-scale warehouse floor prep in the morning and lakefront rust removal in the afternoon — all within the same service area, with no travel fees and no need to transport anything off-site.
