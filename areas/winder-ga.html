Why Winder & Barrow County Need Surface Preparation

Barrow County's economy blends manufacturing, poultry processing, construction, and agriculture — every one of which creates demand for professional surface maintenance. Harrison Poultry in Bethlehem employs over 1,000 people and runs processing facilities with steel structures, concrete floors, and equipment that need regular upkeep. Industrial operations throughout the county maintain warehouses, production equipment, and fleet vehicles that face constant corrosion and coating degradation in Georgia's climate.

Downtown Winder's historic Broad Street and the Jackson Street commercial district — both listed on the National Register of Historic Places — contain brick, metal, and wood structures that need periodic coating removal and surface preparation before restoration work. The 1920 Barrow County Courthouse and surrounding historic buildings are architectural landmarks, but they're also surfaces that weather, corrode, and need professional maintenance to stay intact.

On the rural side, Barrow County still has working farms, timber operations, and agricultural properties with metal buildings, fencing, and heavy equipment that deteriorate faster than most owners realize. Mobile blasting is the only practical option for many of these properties — we bring the rig to your barn, your equipment yard, or your job site and handle the work on location.
