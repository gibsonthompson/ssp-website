Why Oakwood Properties Need Surface Preparation

Oakwood's identity is shifting. What started as a railroad settlement in 1873 has become one of South Hall County's fastest-growing commercial and industrial hubs. The Oakwood South Industrial Park houses manufacturing and distribution operations that depend on maintained infrastructure — steel structures, concrete floors, equipment, and building exteriors that take a beating from daily operations and Georgia's climate.

Along the Mundy Mill Road corridor, retail and commercial properties face the same maintenance cycle: paint fades, coatings crack, and parking lot markings wear down. The Thurmon Tanner Parkway extension has opened new commercial development connecting Oakwood to Flowery Branch, and the planned Oakwood Corporate Greenway along I-985 will bring additional office and industrial space that will need surface preparation from day one.

On the residential side, Oakwood's growth — over 160 new single-family home permits issued in a single recent year — means more homeowners dealing with rusted fences, aging metal structures, and concrete surfaces that need restoration. Our mobile setup is built for exactly this kind of mixed-use community: we handle an industrial warehouse floor in the morning and a residential railing restoration in the afternoon, all within the same zip code.
