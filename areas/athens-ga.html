Why Athens Properties Need Surface Preparation

Athens has a manufacturing heritage that most people don't associate with a college town. The Athena Industrial Park, served directly by CSX and Norfolk Southern freight rail, houses operations ranging from galvanized steel manufacturing to food processing. Caterpillar builds bulldozers here. Since 2019 alone, Athens-Clarke County has attracted over $900 million in investment and thousands of new jobs, with advanced manufacturing driving much of that growth. Every one of these operations relies on properly maintained steel, concrete, and coated surfaces.

Downtown Athens and the surrounding historic districts present a different kind of surface preparation challenge. The city's 15 historic districts contain structures spanning two centuries — brick, iron, wood, and decorative metalwork that need periodic coating removal and restoration. The Bottleworks development on Prince Avenue, the Chase Park Warehouses, and the ongoing adaptive reuse of industrial and commercial buildings throughout the city all require surface prep before renovation work can proceed.

The university's presence adds another layer. UGA's campus and the surrounding commercial infrastructure — restaurants, retail, offices, parking structures — create a steady cycle of maintenance and renovation that includes exterior coating work, concrete restoration, and metal surface repair. Property managers in the university district deal with high turnover and constant wear that demands more frequent surface maintenance than a typical commercial area.
