Why Marietta & Cobb County Need Surface Preparation

Marietta's industrial identity is anchored by aerospace and defense. Lockheed Martin's Marietta facility is one of the largest manufacturing operations in the Southeast, producing military aircraft with a workforce of thousands. Dobbins Air Reserve Base adds another layer of industrial and infrastructure maintenance demand. The support ecosystem around these operations — contractors, suppliers, equipment companies, machine shops — all maintain metal, concrete, and coated surfaces that need regular professional care.

Beyond defense, the I-75 and I-575 corridors through Cobb County carry over 34 million square feet of warehouse, distribution, and manufacturing space extending north through Kennesaw, Acworth, Woodstock, and Cartersville. Concrete floors, structural steel, loading docks, and parking infrastructure across this entire corridor face the same degradation cycle: heat, humidity, industrial use, and time. Mobile blasting handles it all on-site without disrupting operations.

Historic Marietta Square and the surrounding commercial district add demand for building exterior coating removal and surface restoration — brick, metal, and wood structures that need periodic maintenance to preserve their character while staying functional.
