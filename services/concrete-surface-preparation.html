Concrete Surface Preparation

Proper concrete prep is the difference between a coating that lasts 10 years and one that peels in 6 months. We remove contaminants, coatings, and laitance while creating the surface profile your coating system requires.

The Foundation of Every Great Floor Coating

Epoxy, polyurethane, and polyaspartic floor coatings are only as good as the surface beneath them. If the concrete isn't properly prepared — free of contaminants and profiled to the right CSP level — the coating will fail. Delamination, bubbling, and peeling are almost always surface prep failures, not coating failures.

Our abrasive blasting process simultaneously cleans and profiles concrete in a single operation, creating the ideal surface for any coating system.

What We Do

  • Coating Removal — Strip existing epoxy, paint, sealers, and adhesives from concrete floors and surfaces
  • Laitance Removal — Remove the weak cement paste layer from new concrete that prevents coating adhesion
  • Contaminant Removal — Eliminate oil, grease, chemicals, and embedded dirt from industrial and garage floors
  • Surface Profiling — Create the proper Concrete Surface Profile (CSP) required by your coating manufacturer — typically CSP 2-4 for most floor systems
  • Stain & Deposit Removal — Deep cleaning of tire marks, rust stains, efflorescence, and mineral deposits

Who Needs Concrete Prep

  • Floor coating contractors preparing warehouse and commercial floors
  • Property managers recoating parking garage decks and concrete walkways
  • Manufacturing plants resurfacing production floor areas
  • General contractors preparing new concrete for coatings and sealers
  • Auto dealerships and service centers refinishing garage and showroom floors

We Work with Your Coating Contractor

If you already have a coating contractor lined up, we coordinate directly with them to ensure the concrete is prepped to their exact specifications. We understand CSP requirements, moisture concerns, and coating adhesion standards — so the surface we deliver is exactly what your applicator needs.
