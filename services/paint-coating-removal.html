Paint & Coating Removal

Old, failing, or incompatible coatings need to come off completely before new ones go on. We strip paint, powder coating, epoxy, urethane, and any protective coating down to bare substrate.

Strip Any Coating, Any Surface

Whether you're dealing with layers of old lead paint on a historic structure, failed epoxy on a warehouse floor, or flaking powder coat on equipment, our abrasive blasting process removes it completely. No chemical strippers, no heat guns, no tedious manual scraping — just fast, thorough coating removal that leaves the surface clean and properly profiled for the next application.

Coatings We Remove

  • Latex & Oil-Based Paint — Single or multiple layers from metal, wood, concrete, masonry, and brick
  • Powder Coating — Factory-applied finishes on equipment, railings, and manufactured components
  • Epoxy & Urethane — Floor coatings, tank linings, and industrial protective coatings
  • Marine Coatings — Anti-fouling paint, bottom paint, and topcoats from boat hulls
  • Fire-Resistant Coatings — Intumescent and cementitious fireproofing from structural steel
  • Sealers & Waterproofing — Silicone, acrylic, and polyurethane sealers from concrete and masonry

Common Applications

  • Building exterior restoration and re-painting prep
  • Industrial equipment refurbishment
  • Fleet vehicle and trailer repainting
  • Warehouse and garage floor recoating
  • Historic restoration and preservation
  • Bridge and infrastructure maintenance
  • Signage and structural component refinishing

Why Blasting Beats Chemical Stripping

Chemical strippers are slow, messy, create hazardous waste, and often don't reach the substrate completely — especially on textured or porous surfaces. Abrasive blasting removes coatings in a single pass while simultaneously profiling the surface for optimal adhesion of the new coating system. It's faster, cleaner, and produces a better result.
