Striping Removal

Old, faded, or conflicting parking lot stripes create confusion, liability, and an unprofessional appearance. We remove them cleanly so your striping contractor can lay fresh, crisp markings.

Clean Slate for Your Parking Lot

Over time, parking lot striping fades, cracks, and accumulates layers of old paint from repeated re-applications. Eventually, you can't just paint over it anymore — the buildup creates raised edges that peel, the old markings show through, and the whole lot looks sloppy. That's when blasting becomes necessary.

Our mobile abrasive blasting system removes old parking lot striping, traffic markings, directional arrows, handicap stencils, fire lane markings, and curb paint from both asphalt and concrete surfaces. We leave a clean surface that's ready for professional re-striping.

Applications

  • Parking Lot Re-Striping Prep — Remove old lane markings, parking stalls, and directional markings before fresh striping
  • Layout Changes — Remove existing markings when parking lot configurations change (remodels, new traffic flow, ADA updates)
  • Fire Lane & Handicap Updates — Remove and replace fire lane markings, handicap stencils, and loading zone designations to meet current code
  • Road Marking Removal — Remove temporary or permanent road markings from asphalt and concrete roadways
  • Warehouse Floor Markings — Remove old safety lines, forklift lanes, and zone markings in warehouses and distribution centers

Who Uses This Service

  • Property management companies managing retail, office, and residential complexes
  • Striping contractors who need surfaces prepped before laying new markings
  • General contractors handling parking lot renovation projects
  • Municipalities and DOT for road marking updates
  • Warehouse and distribution center operators

Why Blasting vs. Grinding

Grinding is the traditional method for stripe removal, but it's slower, creates more dust, leaves visible grind marks, and can damage the asphalt or concrete surface. Abrasive blasting removes the paint without cutting into the substrate, leaving a cleaner result in less time. For large parking lots and multi-property managers, the speed difference alone makes blasting the better choice.
