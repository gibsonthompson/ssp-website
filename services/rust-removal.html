Rust Removal

Rust compromises structural integrity, accelerates deterioration, and prevents coating adhesion. We eliminate it completely, leaving clean bare metal ready for primer or protective coating.

Complete Rust Elimination

Rust isn't just unsightly — it's actively destroying your metal assets. Left untreated, surface rust becomes pitting corrosion, weakening structural steel, degrading equipment, and causing premature failure. Our abrasive blasting process removes rust completely, down to clean bare metal, so protective coatings can bond properly and last.

We blast to SSPC surface preparation standards, including SP-6 (Commercial Blast), SP-10 (Near-White Metal), and SP-5 (White Metal) depending on your coating requirements and the application environment.

What We Remove Rust From

  • Structural Steel — I-beams, columns, trusses, and support structures in commercial and industrial buildings
  • Heavy Equipment — Excavators, loaders, trailers, agricultural implements, and fleet vehicles
  • Railings & Fencing — Wrought iron, steel, and aluminum railings, gates, and fences
  • Storage Tanks — Water tanks, fuel tanks, chemical storage tanks (interior and exterior)
  • Pipe & Piping Systems — Exposed pipe runs, pipe racks, and pipeline surfaces
  • Metal Siding & Roofing — Corrugated metal, standing seam, and panel systems
  • Marine & Automotive — Boat hulls, vehicle frames, chassis, and body panels

The Problem with Painting Over Rust

Applying primer or paint over rust is a temporary fix that fails quickly. The rust continues to grow underneath the new coating, causing blistering, flaking, and delamination — often within months. Proper surface preparation through abrasive blasting removes the rust entirely and creates a surface profile that mechanical bonds with the coating system, ensuring a finish that lasts years instead of months.

Our Process

We start by assessing the severity of corrosion and the requirements of your planned coating system. From there, we select the appropriate media and blast to the required SSPC standard. After blasting, the window for applying primer or coating is time-sensitive — we coordinate with your painting contractor or coating applicator to ensure the surface stays clean and ready.
